Linear Regression Model to predict Math Score in Phoenix Schools

  • Tech Stack: Python, Machine Learninig
  • Github URL: Project Link

The linear regression model I developed focuses on predicting the score of schools in Phoenix based on 10 different attributes. This project leverages the power of statistical modeling to understand and analyze the factors that contribute to the overall performance of schools in the Phoenix area.

The 10 attributes used in this model encompass various aspects of schools, including student-teacher ratio, average class size, percentage of students receiving free or reduced-price lunch, teacher qualification levels, school funding, facilities quality, student attendance rates, and academic performance metrics such as standardized test scores in math and language arts.

The model utilizes linear regression, a fundamental statistical technique, to establish relationships between these attributes and the overall score of schools. By fitting a linear equation to the data, the model aims to estimate the effect of each attribute on the school's score, providing insights into which factors are most influential in determining school performance.

To develop and validate the model, I collected a comprehensive dataset comprising information on these attributes for a diverse set of schools in the Phoenix area. The dataset was then divided into training and testing sets to ensure the model's generalizability and accuracy.

During the training phase, the model learned the underlying patterns and correlations within the data, adjusting its parameters to minimize the difference between predicted and actual school scores. The model's performance was evaluated using metrics such as mean squared error or R-squared to assess its predictive accuracy and reliability.
