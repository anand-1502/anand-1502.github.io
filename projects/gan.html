Image Generator

  • Tech Stack: Tensorflow, Python, numpy, keras, pandas
  • Github URL: Project Link

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) are a class of artificial intelligence algorithms used in unsupervised machine learning, implemented by a system of two neural networks contesting with each other in a zero-sum game framework. The generator network creates fake data, while the discriminator network tries to distinguish between real and fake data. They are trained together in a process called adversarial training until the generator generates realistic data that can fool the discriminator.

In the context of the provided statement, an image generator based on GANs was developed with the objective of producing realistic images that closely resemble those from a real dataset. This system was specifically tested using a human-face database, where the generator network was trained to create fake images of human faces.

To assess the performance of the image generator, a metric known as the Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was used. PCA is a statistical technique used to simplify data sets by reducing the number of variables while preserving the most important aspects of the data. In this case, the PCA of both the generated images and the original images from the dataset were computed and compared. By evaluating the similarity between the PCAs of the generated and original images, the effectiveness of the image generator in producing realistic images could be determined.
