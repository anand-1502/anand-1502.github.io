Stock Price Predicition

  • Tech Stack: Python

Stock price prediction using GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit) and LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) is a popular application of recurrent neural networks (RNNs) in the field of finance and machine learning. GRU and LSTM are types of RNNs that are well-suited for sequential data processing tasks due to their ability to retain long-term dependencies in the data.

In this context, the GRU and LSTM neural network architectures are used to analyze historical stock price data and predict future stock prices. The historical stock price data typically includes factors such as opening price, closing price, highest price, lowest price, and trading volume over a specific time period.

The neural network model is trained on historical stock price data to learn patterns and relationships within the data. This training involves adjusting the parameters of the GRU and LSTM layers to minimize the difference between the predicted stock prices and the actual stock prices in the training dataset. Once trained, the model can then be used to make predictions on new, unseen data.

Evaluation of the model's performance is typically done using metrics such as Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), or Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) by comparing the predicted stock prices with the actual stock prices from a test dataset.
